Utilize Coalition · $100B grid utilization opportunity · Brattle Group, 2024 · Open Google Form survey · No personal data collected

The U.S. grid runs at 53% capacity. 61% of households say electricity costs too much. Only 22% of smart thermostat owners are enrolled in demand response programs that would lower those bills. The gap is not access or technology — it is adoption, and it fails at a specific moment, for a specific reason, that is different for every person. This research identifies where and why.

Key findings
Wave 1 primary finding
Renters are not in Stage 1 or Stage 2 — they are in a pre-stage where the decision framework does not apply
The adoption model assumes the person has authority to act. Wave 1 broke that assumption. Renters were not uninformed or skeptical — they were structurally excluded. The decision was not theirs to make. This changed the routing logic: the tool now acknowledges structural exclusion before offering any path, because respondents in this segment disengage before reaching a CTA.
"Our apartment is tied up with APS — we don't have another option." · W1-R06
"This would really be my landlord's decision." · W1-R08
Finding 2
Upfront cost and cost-uncertainty block 8 of 20 respondents
3 of 20 named cost directly. 5 more cited cost-adjacent hesitations — HOA approval uncertainty, conflicting quotes, income constraints. The savings estimator is the primary conversion mechanism for this group, not secondary.
"Two kids so big purchases feel risky." · R01
Finding 3
High-intent respondents are stalled by complexity — not cost
R02, R05, and R11 — motivated, informed, stalled. None cite cost. All cite process complexity or time burden. The wedge is a simplified first step, not more information.
"I get analysis paralysis." · R02
Finding 4
Outage concern is an underlayer motivation across markets
8 of 20 rated outage concern ≥3 of 5 across multiple states. Resilience framing appeared in open-ended triggers even when not the stated primary hesitation — not only FL/TX.
Finding 5
Bad install experiences create durable Stage 2 skeptics
R07 stalled on a battery purchase after a bad solar install with no follow-up support. Multiple respondents cited past negative experiences as the reason they stopped researching. Blocked by trust, not cost.
"The experience was bad enough that I've stalled on the battery I actually want." · R07
